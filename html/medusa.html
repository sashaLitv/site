MEDUSA

Medusa was once a beautiful maiden who served as a priestess in Athena's temple. After being cursed by Athena for breaking her vows, Medusa's hair was turned into snakes, and she gained the power to turn anyone who gazed at her into stone. Her tragic tale remains one of Greek mythology's most captivating legends.

"Medusa's Transformation"... "Perseus and Medusa's Demise"...
Medusa was once a beautiful maiden who served as a priestess in Athena's temple. Poseidon, captivated by her beauty, desecrated the temple by pursuing Medusa. In her rage, Athena punished Medusa instead of Poseidon, transforming her hair into snakes and cursing her with the power to turn anyone who gazed at her into stone.
Perseus, a Greek hero, was sent to slay Medusa as part of his quest. With the help of Athena and Hermes, he obtained items like a mirrored shield and winged sandals, allowing him to defeat Medusa without gazing at her directly. Medusa's head later became a powerful artifact.
