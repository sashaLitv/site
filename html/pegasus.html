PEGASUS

Pegasus, the winged horse, was born from the blood of Medusa after her demise at the hands of Perseus. This mythical creature symbolizes freedom and grace, often associated with the muses and inspiration.

"Birth of Pegasus"... "Bellerophon and Pegasus"...
Pegasus, the winged horse, was born from the blood of Medusa after Perseus beheaded her. As her divine offspring with Poseidon, Pegasus symbolized strength and inspiration, taking flight immediately after his birth.
The hero Bellerophon tamed Pegasus with the help of Athena's golden bridle. Together, they defeated the Chimera, a fearsome fire-breathing creature. However, Bellerophon’s hubris led him to attempt to fly to Mount Olympus on Pegasus. Zeus punished this arrogance by causing Pegasus to buck, throwing Bellerophon back to Earth. Pegasus, however, was welcomed to Olympus and served Zeus by carrying his thunderbolts.
